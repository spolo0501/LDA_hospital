```markdown
3.3 Determining the Optimal Number of Topics

The selection of the optimal number of topics (K) is crucial for
the validity of LDA analysis. We employed a multi-criteria approach
combining quantitative metrics and qualitative assessment.

First, we systematically trained 14 LDA models with K ranging from
2 to 15, evaluating each model using:

(1) Coherence Score (C_v): Measures the semantic consistency of
    words within topics (RÃ¶der et al., 2015). Higher values indicate
    better topic interpretability.

(2) Perplexity: Measures the statistical fit of the model. Lower
    values indicate better model performance.

Second, we examined the coherence-topic curve to identify potential
elbow points, where increasing K yields diminishing returns in
coherence improvement.

Third, we conducted qualitative assessment of topic keywords for
interpretability:
- Semantic clarity: Are the top words in each topic coherent?
- Topic distinction: Do topics have minimal keyword overlap?
- Theoretical alignment: Do topics align with service quality theory?

Based on these criteria, K=5 was selected as optimal, despite K=2
achieving the highest coherence score (0.4641). The rationale for
this decision is threefold:

(1) Statistical performance: K=5 maintained high coherence (0.4326,
    ranked 3rd), indicating good topic quality.

(2) Interpretability: K=2 only distinguished positive vs. negative
    sentiments, lacking the granularity necessary for actionable
    managerial insights. K=5 revealed distinct, non-overlapping
    service dimensions (keywords overlap <15%).

(3) Theoretical validity: K=5 aligns with established service
    quality frameworks (e.g., SERVQUAL's 5 dimensions; Parasuraman
    et al., 1988) and healthcare service quality literature
    (Dagger et al., 2007).

(4) Balanced distribution: Topics ranged from 12.9% to 28.7% of
    reviews, avoiding extreme concentration or fragmentation.

This decision reflects the principle that optimal K balances
statistical optimization with substantive interpretability
(Griffiths & Steyvers, 2004).
```

```markdown
While K=2 achieved the highest coherence score (0.464), we rejected

insights. Hospital managers cannot improve "negative sentiment"
without knowing which specific service aspects (e.g., waiting time,
staff attitude) require attention.

Therefore, we prioritized interpretability and practical utility
over pure statistical optimization, consistent with best practices
in topic modeling (Chang et al., 2009).
```

```markdown
service quality research.
```
