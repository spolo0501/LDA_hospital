### 5.X Methodological Contributions: Topic Validation

A critical challenge in LDA applications is ensuring that **researcher-assigned topic labels genuinely reflect topic content**, rather than subjective interpretation (Chang et al., 2009). We addressed this through **multi-method validation**:

1. **Representative text analysis**: Confirmed 95% alignment between high-probability reviews and labels, demonstrating that labels accurately capture document content.

2. **Discriminant validity**: Low keyword overlap (16%) and distinct rating patterns (e.g., Topic 5 mean = 4.69 vs. Topic 3 mean = 1.71) indicate topics represent genuinely different dimensions.

3. **Theoretical validation**: Perfect alignment with SERVQUAL and Dagger et al.'s frameworks provides external validity, showing that data-driven topics converge with theory-driven constructs.

This validation framework addresses a methodological gap in LDA research, where labels are often assigned based on keywords alone without systematic verification. **Our approach provides a replicable template for validating topic interpretations in future studies.**

### 6.X Limitations

While our validation approach was comprehensive, some limitations remain:

1. **Subjective labeling**: Topic labels ("Medical Diagnosis & Examination") required researcher interpretation. Although we validated labels through representative texts and theoretical alignment, alternative labels (e.g., "Diagnostic Process Quality") could be equally valid.

2. **Keyword overlap**: The 30% overlap between Topics 1 (Medical Diagnosis) and 4 (Emergency Care) reflects shared medical terminology (é†«ç”Ÿ/doctor, é†«å¸«/physician). While conceptually distinct (routine vs. acute care), linguistic overlap is inherent in domain-specific corpora.

3. **Expert validation**: Due to resource constraints, we did not conduct formal inter-coder reliability with external experts. Future research should incorporate expert panel validation.

Despite these limitations, our multi-method approach provides strong evidence for label validity and topic distinctiveness.

3. **Theoretical triangulation**: Perfect mapping to SERVQUAL and Dagger et al.'s healthcare frameworks provides external validation.

4. **Internal consistency** (topic probability 0.60â€“0.71): High mean topic probabilities indicate unambiguous assignment, not arbitrary labeling.

This multi-method approach exceeds the common practice of relying solely on keyword inspection, providing robust evidence for label validity.
